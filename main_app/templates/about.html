Welcome to Big Cat Liberation

What we Do

Our main mission is to Provide care and abundant habitats for large feline species to live in. Big cats should never be raised as pets but we believe everyone deserves a second change, especially the ones that can't speak for themselves. This is where our Big Cat Surrender Project comes into play. Through Big Cat Liberation, we seek individuals around the country to relinquish their big cats to us so we could offer them a scond chance in life with professional care and environment most fitting to their natural needs.

Why are there so many Big Cat Sanctuaries in the US?

There are as many as 10,000 big cats living in captvity in the US. These animals often live in terrible conditions: from private yards to unqualified roadside zoos. When big cats are confiscated, a lot of effort goes into finding them the proper placement and luckily, over the years, more and more sanctuaries are created to meet those needs. Big Cat Liberation is one of the recent ones to join the fight to end exotic animal ownership.

The sanctuary is not open for public visitation as we're in the middle of expanding our Big Cat Area.
